BAMRU Web Infrastructure
BAMRU is a search and rescue team of volunteers who have found hundreds of missing people, young and old, in life-changing events that affect families and communities. Learn more at http://bamru.org.

BAMRU Web Infrastructure
Web-infrastructure is critical to our operations. BAMRU relies on a variety of tools, from database-driven administrative apps to mobile alerting apps.

But as a non-profit, BAMRU has no staff or budget. We have built our tools over the years with a volunteer group of technical collaborators.

BAMRU always needs technical people who are interested in helping to maintain and extend our tools. Each contribution makes a big impact for our organization.

Who can help?
BAMRU has a variety of potential projects (listed below). We can adjust the scope of any project to match the abilities/interests of the contributor. We seek a fit where the developer has high enthusiasm for the project.

The best fit will be BAMRU members or outside individuals with a background in electronics, networking, software development, web marketing or graphic design. Our projects are great for local college students in advanced computer science classes.

To get involved contact Andy Leak - 650-823-0836 - andy@r210.com

BAMRU Projects
1. SMARTPHONE PAGING APP - Create an Android/IOS app that would supplement our current email/SMS paging alerts. The app must make a loud noise to wake members for middle-of-the-night callouts. Technical skills: PhoneGap, HTML/CSS/JS, WebSockets, Git, Backbone.js, light Ruby/Rails.

2. WEB MARKETING - Update our public website style and add data entry forms for recruiting and fundraising prospects. Add tracking analytics, and launch an Adwords campaign ($10K/month budget). Measure and report the results. Technical skills: HTML/CSS/JS, Git, light Ruby/Rails, Twitter Bootstrap, Google AdWords & Analytics.

3. VOICE CONFERENCING SYSTEM - Use WebRTC to create a web-based voice conferencing system that can be used by both desktop and mobile clients. Integrate the conferencing system to BAMRU's paging system. Technical Skills: HTML/CSS/JS, WebRTC, Ruby/Rails, Git

4. MOBILE SERVER / HOTSPOT - Help to create a mobile app server that will be used at remote command post locations. Technical Skills: WiFi Pineapple, Small Linux Servers (BeagleBone/RasberryPi/Parallella), GPS/3G integration, Database replication, Ruby/Rails.

5. MOBILE WIKI - Adapt and customize Ward Cunningham's 'Smallest Federated Wiki' for use within the BAMRU Intranet. Technical Skills: CoffeeScript, Backbone, Sinatra, HTML/CSS.

Our Approach
For all of our projects, we value simple solutions that deliver a real benefit and are easy to operate and maintain. Contributors must be self-motivated and comfortable with an open-source work style. All contributions are welcome, big or small.
